--- title: 3 Types of Probability layout: post category: math author: Statistics excerpt: Simple explanation for Marginal, Joint, and Conditional probability ---

Marginal Probability: P(A)

The probability that an event will occur. It is not conditioned by another event. That's really it.

Joint Probability: P(A ∩ B)

The probability that an event will occur AND another event will occur.

Conditional Probability: P(A | B)

The probability that an event will occur given that we already know a different event has occured. The probability that A will occur given that we've witnessed B occur is P(A ∩ B) / P(B). That's the joint probability / marginal probability of the event we already witnessed. In plain english its the probability of events A and B happening divided by the chance of B happening in general.

Application

These probabilities are the building blocks of Baye's Rule
